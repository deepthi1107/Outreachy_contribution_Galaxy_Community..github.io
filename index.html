Overview

Climate can be defined as average weather over an area for a longer period of time. It is classified into six types: tropical rainy, dry, temperate marine, temperate continental, polar, and highlands. The climate depends upon geographical scale, topography, and other factors. Similarly, India has six types of climate are deserts, alpine tundra, glaciers, humid tropical, rain forests, and islands. Habitat, living style, and health depend on the climatic type we live in. Studies show that India’s temperature has risen by 0.7 °C between 1901 and 2018 which has an impact on climatic conditions. Some of the predicted effects are landslides and floods that might increase in Assam, sea-level rise in northeastern states, and melting of glaciers that increase the water flow of Ganga, Brahmaputra, and the Yamuna, effect on agriculture, increase in heatwaves.
